CEMACS Experiment
Atmosphere-sea flux exchanges power the weather and climate engines and the global cabon-moisture cycle. Though carbon emissions have increased significantly for the past half-century, scientists found that the sea carbon uptake have more than doubled. However, climate models predict a reduction in the sea carbon uptake due to increased sea surface temperature caused by climate change. Direct carbon and moisture flux measurments can reveal the mechanism responsible for these uptakes, which is critical in improving the climate models.
